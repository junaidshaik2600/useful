> Second — preparing weekly quality reports.
> Third — daily communication like SOD huddles and MOM emails.
> Fourth — one-on-one feedback using structured methods.
> Fifth — supporting OJT batches.
> Additionally, QAs handle client escalations through RCA and conduct refresher trainings.”

---

## ❌ BAD

> “Precision is TP by TP+FP”

## ✅ FIXED

> “Precision measures how accurate our suspensions are.
> Formula is TP divided by TP plus FP.
> In our workflow, it means — out of all accounts we marked as bad, how many were actually bad.
> If precision drops, it indicates over-flagging, so I would focus on reducing false positives through targeted feedback.”

---

## ❌ BAD

> “RCA is root cause analysis”

## ✅ FIXED

> “RCA is Root Cause Analysis used to identify the actual reason behind an error.
> Instead of correcting only the mistake, we analyze why it happened using methods like 5Y analysis.
> As a QA, I would prepare RCA for client escalations, identify whether it is a reviewer or system issue, and take corrective action to prevent recurrence.”
